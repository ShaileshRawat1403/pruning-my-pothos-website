title: 'Confidence Is Cheap. Understanding Is Costly.'
tags:

Confidence is now mass-produced. Ask a question and a clean, well-formed answer arrives in seconds. It feels authoritative. It feels *real*.

But what we receive is not truth, only plausibility. These systems assemble patterns into something that sounds right. The smoother the answer, the easier it is to accept.

Understanding is slower. It requires friction, mistakes, and time. It is the cost of building a mental model you can trust.

AI makes repetition effortless and execution easy. That speed is useful, but it also tempts us to skip the hard work of judgment.
